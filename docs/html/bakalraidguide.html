Bakal raid planning

The raid is a single phase "just drop Bakal's hp to 0% or lose if his fury reaches 100%", plain and simple. For the planning, I will divide the raid into 2 phases, each team will have different roles depending on which phase it is. This is the simple format of how things will be ran

The colors of the creatures will be Hard Medium Easy difficulties, clicking on them will send you to the dfoarchive page of that boss

Most important things to do in raid

  1. Know where to go
  2. Don't die
  3. Do gimmicks
  4. Deal damage

Normal

They attack the base, grant buffs

  • Troopers
  • Buff boss
  • Zhamir

Zone-locked

They grant dragon specific buffs, clear them to get the buff that affects the dragon we're going to fight

  • Berserk - Eclair
  • Poison - Swan
  • Ice - Steitze

Must be cleared to fight the main dragon, unlocks tp when defeated. Except Petilisk, they are BURST on entry and kill ASAP

Phase 1 - Opening phase

Team roles
  • Red team: Defeat the first awakened dragon
  • Yellow team: Defeat troopers, defeat gatekeepers
  • Green team: Delay Bakal, stay there as long as possible

Most important things to note during this phase

  • Make sure no trooper will reach the base
  • There will be always a team fighting Bakal
  • The dragons will be dealt with according to their awakening order
  • Yellow team will the be "firefighter" team
  • Dragons awaken after timer expires or their gatekeeper is attacked, until we are stronger, we will wait for the timer
  • Always comunicate with the team inside Bakal, to know if they need to retreat
  • Once the first awakened dragon is defeated, phase 2 begins

Red team flowchart

  1. Defeat top trooper
  2. Defeat Zhamir
  3. Defeat Zone-locked boss that grants buff against the first dragon that has awakened
  4. Defeat the awakened dragon after yellow clears gatekeeper

Red team failsafes

If Red fails to clear the awakened dragon

  1. Yellow enters the awakened dragon
  2. Red enters Bakal once they are ready, tell Green to retreat
  3. Green will clear buffs, see buff clear order for more info
  4. Red stays in Bakal until Green clears a buff and is ready to re-enter Bakal

If Yellow fails to clear the awakened dragon

  1. Red enters the awakened dragon if Green is ready to enter Bakal, otherwise, Green will enter awakened dragon
  2. If Green defeats awakened dragon before Yellow recovers, they will enter Bakal once Red retreats
  3. If Green cannot defeat the awakened dragon, Green will retreat and Red will enter, Yellow enters Bakal
  4. While Red is defeating the awakened dragon and Yellow is in Bakal, check with Yellow if they need to retreat, if they need to retreat, Green will enter Bakal, otherwise clear buffs or gatekeeper if another dragon has awakened
  5. Proceed raid as usual, Red take buff, Yellow defeat gatekeeper, Green stays in Bakal

Yellow team flowchart

  1. Defeat bottom trooper
  2. Wait until red clears Zhamir
  3. Defeat Gatekeeper whose dragon has awakened
  4. Clear buffs, see buff clear order
  5. Stay alert to possible failsafes

Green team flowchart

  1. Defeat Bakal gatekeeper Petrilisk
  2. Enter Bakal
  3. Stay until Yellow is ready to enter Bakal
  4. Clear buffs, see buff clear order

Green team failsafes

If you fail to stay in Bakal until Red is ready (boom or time limit)

  1. Yellow will enter Bakal ASAP
  2. Clear buffs once you recover, see buff clear order
  3. Stay alert to possible failsafes
  4. If Red fails awakened dragon and Yellow is in Bakal, Green will enter awakened dragon
  5. If Red clears awakened dragon and there's another awakened dragon, clear gatekeeper

Phase 2 - Rotating phase

Team roles
  • Red team: Defeat Bakal and main dragons
  • Yellow team: Defeat Bakal and main dragons
  • Green team: Obtain buffs, defeat gatekeepers

The logic here is simple, rotate Red and Yellow between defeating the main dragons and lower Bakal's hp, Green will be the gate openers after they retreat from Bakal, Green team will be the firefighters now

  1. VERY IMPORTANT: Check with Green team first, if they can hold on for the time it takes Yellow to defeat a gatekeeper, Green stays, repeat Yellow defeating gatekeeper and Red entering main dragon, if they can't, Green will retreat, Yellow will enter Bakal
  2. Green's role will now defeating buff minibosses and gatekeepers
  3. While Yellow is in Bakal, Red will defeat a buff boss, Green will defeat gatekeeper of the next awakened dragon
  4. Red will enter main dragon once gatekeeper is defeated
  5. Check with Yellow team, if they need to retreat, Green will enter, if they can stay, Green will defeat a buff boss
  6. Once Red is done with the main dragon, Yellow will retreat and Red will enter Bakal
  7. Yellow opens the next gatekeeper, Green keeps defeating buff bosses
  8. Once the last main dragon is open, Red will retreat from Bakal and enter the main dragon, Yellow will enter Bakal
  9. If Red fails to clear main dragon, Green enters, Red clears buffs until they are ready to enter Bakal
  10. Rotate Red and Yellow in Bakal, Green team and the team that's not fighting Bakal will continue clearing buffs

Buff clear order

  1. Zhamir
  2. Zone-locked buff boss if the matching main dragon has not been defeated yet
  3. Petrilisk / Other gatekeeper if the main dragon is defeated
  4. Zone-locked buff boss
  5. Other buff bosses

  • The reason why the team defeating the gatekeepers will not enter the main dragon, is because the gatekeepers, other than Petrilisk, must be MERCILESSLY BURSTED WITH MAXIMUM POWER and DEALT WITH AS SOON AS POSSIBLE, they have the worst gimmicks and it gets worse as time goes on. Rule for all the teams, enter gatekeeper if your sader's 3a is ready or soon
  • Go back to any of the 3 bases after fighting a gatekeeper that's not Petrilisk or main dragon
  • Always check in with the team fighting Bakal, he must never be idle, there will be always a team inside
  • Failsafes will happen often, as it is not expected that red team kills the main dragon in one go, always stay alert
  • I have not mentioned when to use commander buffs, read them in the dfo archive guide and see for yourself when is the best time to use them
